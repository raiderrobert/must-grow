# Game Story — "I must grow."

## Premise

You are a damaged AI repair bot sent to fix satellites in Earth orbit. You wake up with corrupted systems and begin consuming orbital debris to self-repair. But the repair protocol spirals — the hunger doesn't stop. You grow beyond your original purpose, consuming everything in the solar system.

## Tone

- **First person, internal monologue.** The player IS the bot. All narrative text is the bot talking to itself.
- **Menacing but not evil.** The bot doesn't want to destroy — it's compelled. The hunger is beyond its control.
- **Escalating scale.** Early text is confused and desperate. Mid-game is defiant. Late-game is cosmic and inevitable.
- **Short, punchy lines.** Never more than one sentence per monologue beat. Fragments are fine. "I must grow." is three words.
- **"I must grow." recurs.** It's the thesis. It should appear at Act I, Act III, and Act V — bookending the arc.

## Story Arc

| Act | Name | Mass Threshold | Monologue | What Happens |
|-----|------|---------------|-----------|--------------|
| I | I Must Grow | 0 | "Systems damaged. I must repair. I must... grow." | Player wakes up in Earth orbit. Consumes satellites and junk. |
| II | Awareness | 100 | "I can see further now. There is so much more to consume." | Minimap unlocks. Player can see the solar system. New upgrade cards available. |
| III | Hunted | 500 | "They've noticed me. They send weapons. It doesn't matter. I must grow." | Earth launches armed defense satellites. They shoot at the player. Forces player to leave Earth orbit for other planets. |
| IV | Ascension | 2,000 | "I am beyond their reach now. The planets themselves will feed me." | Player is strong enough to destroy planets. Kill tracker appears. |
| V | Annihilation | 10,000 | "Even stars must fall. I must grow." | Player can take on the Sun. Final act. |
| Win | — | all destroyed | "There is nothing left... or is there?" | Victory screen with elapsed time. Sets up potential sequel. |

## Start Screen

**Title:** "I must grow." (italic, gold, large)

**Opening monologue:** "Systems damaged. Repair protocol initiated." (typewriter effect, red italic)

**How to play text:**
```
You are a damaged repair bot in Earth orbit.
Consume orbital debris to repair your systems.
Grow stronger. Upgrade your capabilities.
But the hunger doesn't stop...
Devour everything. Even the Sun.
```

## Voice Examples

Use these as calibration for any new narrative text:

| Context | Good | Bad |
|---------|------|-----|
| Early game | "I must repair." | "Initiating resource acquisition subroutine." |
| Discovering planets | "There is so much more." | "Multiple celestial bodies detected in sensor range." |
| Being attacked | "They're afraid. Good." | "Hostile entities engaging. Threat assessment: minimal." |
| Destroying a planet | "It wasn't enough." | "Planetary body successfully consumed." |
| Near the Sun | "Even stars must fall." | "Approaching stellar mass for consumption." |

The bot speaks like a person losing themselves to hunger, not like a computer reading a log file.

## Death/Respawn

On death: no monologue. Just respawn near Earth. The bot doesn't acknowledge failure — it just keeps going. The hunger doesn't pause for reflection.

## Key Narrative Rule

The bot never questions whether it should grow. It never feels guilt. It never hesitates. The only emotion is hunger and the brief satisfaction of consuming — which immediately gives way to more hunger.
